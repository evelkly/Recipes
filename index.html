Beef

Beef Wellington

Looking for an impressive dish for the holidays? It takes a little time to put this dish together, but it's simple to do... and the results are oustanding and delicious.

SERVINGS
10 		PREP TIME
1 hrs 25 mins 		TOTAL TIME
2 hrs 50 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 beef tenderloin (2 to 2.5 lbs)
  • ground black pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 17.3 ounce package Pepperidge Farm Puff Pastry Sheets (1 sheet), thawed
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 cups finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped (about 1/2 cup)

Directions

  1. Heat the oven to 425 degrees F. Place the beef into a lightly greased roasting pan. Season with the black pepper if desired. Roast for 30 minutes or until a meat thermometer reads 130 degrees F. Cover the pan and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Heat the oven to 425 degrees F. Beat the egg and water in a small bowl with a fork or whisk.
  3. Heat th ebutter in a 10-inch skillet over medium-high heat. Add the mushrooms and onion and cook until the mushrooms are tender and all the liquid is evaporated, stirring often.
  4. Unfold the pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface. Roll the pastry sheet into a rectangle 4 inches longer and 6 inches wider than the beef. Brush the pastry sheet with the egg mixture. Spoon the mushroom mixture onto the pastry sheet to within 1-inch of the edges. Place the beef in the center of the mushroom mixture. Fold the pastry over the beef and press to seal. Place seam-side down onto a baking sheet. Tuck the ends under to seal. Brush the pastry with the egg mixture.
  5. Bake for 25 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown and a meat thermometer reads 140 degrees F.
Serving Suggestion:
Serve with green beans amandine. For dessert serve with cheesecake topped with sliced strawberries.

Chicken

Filipino Chicken BBQ

Description

SERVINGS
6-8 		PREP TIME
3-4 hours 		TOTAL TIME
10 mins

Ingredients

  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 bouillon cube
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 lbs chicken thigs, cut into samll pieces

Directions

  1. Combine all the ingredients except the chicken to make a marmalade
  2. Marinate the chicken in the marmalade for 3-4 hours
  3. Start a charcoal grill and let the coals get very hot. You want smoking, flameless coals.
  4. Thread the chicken pieces onto wood skewers and place on a metal grate over the coals.
  5. Brush with oil and grill until the chicken is cooked through, about 5 minutes.Remove from the heat and let them reast a few minutes
  6. Repeat the grilling process with the cooked chicken, brushing with oil as necessary, until the outside of the chicken is a deep golden brown with a crispy exterior.
Serving suggestions:
Dip in a soy sauce and vinegar mixture with bird's eye chilis for a little spiciness.

Pork

Pork Asado

Description

SERVINGS
6 		PREP TIME
10 mins 		TOTAL TIME
1 hr 10 mins

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs pork shoulder
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons garlic salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 knorr chicken bouillon cube
  • 1 pinch ground cloves (tiny pinch)
  • 2 cups water

Directions

  1. Cut the pork into 1/2 inch cubes removing any fat
  2. Heat a large pot on medium high and fry the pork allowing it to fry in its own juices.
  3. After the meat is browned on all sides, turn the heat to medium loq and cover, stirring occassional until the meat is fork tender, about 45 minutes
  4. Add flour to the pork and stir lightly, browning the flour for 2 minutes
  5. Finally, add the tomato sauce, all of the spices and water. Stir to combine and cook on medium heat just until the sauce thickens.
  6. Add more garlic salt to taste and chili powder if needed. If sauce gets too thick, you can add another 1/4 cup water until the sauce has the consistency you like. It should resemble a runny gravy.

Monkey Bread

The classic monkey bread recipe, oozing with warm caramel and cinnamon!

SERVINGS
6 - 8 		PREP TIME
10 mins 		TOTAL TIME
45 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 rolls of Pillsbury biscuits
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 3 tablespoons cinnamon
  • 1 stick butter

Directions

  1. Tear biscuits into quarters
  2. Mix sugar, brown sugar and cinnamon into a plastic bag
  3. Drop each quarter into the bag and shake well to coat
  4. Place the quarters into a dutch oven
  5. Melt butter and pour over the biscuits
  6. Bake at 350 degrees F for 35 minutes
Suggestions:
Line the dutch oven with foil or dutch oven liners to catch melted sugar for easier cleanup.
Use a charcoal chimey to reduce the prep time for heating up the charcoal
